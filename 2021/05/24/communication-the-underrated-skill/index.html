Communication the Underrated Skill

Mon May 24 2021

If someone one day asks me, what is the core ability to execute a project properly. My answer will be first Communication, second Communication, third Communication…

This is the most complex skill that a team shall develop and commonly is confused with high-level skills like Trust, Engagement, Proactivity, Leadership, and so on. And funnily, communication is the most underrated skill due to this quite often is considered the level entry ability in every high performer project executer.

But, first, why am I referring to a project executer more than a project manager or project leader? is because the communication skill shall be transversal along with all the project participants, and in effect, if one part of the team has not properly communications skills developed is highly probable that they are running in difficulties or go-ahead to live in pain.

For understanding what means communication, we can use the Google definition:

COMMUNICATION
: [noun]
: 1. the imparting or exchanging of information by speaking, writing or using some other medium
2. means of sending or receiving information, such as phone lines or computers.

I am not agreed with the previous definition, because often we are assuming that the act of communication happens with the mere action of spread the message, which can be fit for general purpose communication like an everyday announcement that is the responsibility of the receptor filtrate and understands the message.

For a commonplace, we can define communication as the act of sharing information assuring the understanding for each part of the communication process.

WTF?

In simple words, you need to be sure that your message is passed properly and you need to be sure that you understand the message that someone gives to you.

Some examples of bad or poor communication:

  • Send an email to inform/report something important and assume that is all.
  • In a multi-discipline meeting raise a warning flag and assume that all participants understand the same as you.
  • Say to all the things yes without analysis of the collaterals and leftovers (always happens in meetings with clients)
  • Take risk hoping that someone in the future will deal with hour silly decision.
  • etc.

To the surprise of all the readers this issue is more common than it seems and often the lack of communication is not perceived for the project executer at fault.

Some key factors that help to improve our project communication skills are:

  1. Be kind ( Not always you have the truth, no matter the years of experience)
  2. Develop oratory skills ( ability to talk in public)
  3. Be a good teller ( Always a good history is better than a bad one)
  4. Be clear, analyze your speech, email, report with the eyes of a rookie.
  5. Do not be afraid to say “I do not understand”, Do not be tough if someone does not understand.
  6. Learn, learn a lot, learn of everything, often learn new things improve the ability to make the right questions (a key skill of a good leader)
